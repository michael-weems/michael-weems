    Software Dev

    I've been doing full-stack web-dev for most of my career. Started in Java with Jersey Servlets, MySQL, and GlassFish. Moved to Node.js and Angular for a long while. Eventually ported to Go and HTMX a while back. I've done most things unrelated to actually connecting to the real internet, as my apps deploy on intranets. Stuff like: creating custom CSS design systems, CRUD forms, windows installers, CI / CD, creating build systems, database architecture, some data-processing w/ concurrency, team management and mentorship, etc...

    Lately I've been excited to dig into game development. The chasm of technical concepts to explore is severely deep and broad - but I'm eager to pull out my spelunking gear and do the exploration. The fact that I can combine my music and programming passions to create something lights a fire in my soul to get at it. I'd like to also learn the art and game-design side. If I end up with a career in game-dev or something else or stay in my current career trajectory, either way I'll most-likely be building my projects at home.

    Tools

    My preferred dev setup includes NeoVim, really any Linux distro, Hyprland, and Tmux. I use the MoErgo Glove80 keyboard.

    Music

    I grew up singing and playing the drums, and in the last several years I've had the absolute joy of playing with several other local musicians. We primarily do improvised jam-sessions where we'll play anything from prog jazz to metal to blues to pirate shanties. My next route is to get into audio engineering / production to mix and master tracks we record. My latest obsession is guitar pedals. I love hooking them up to a variety of instruments and getting completely different sound profiles. Like hooking up reverb to my drums to get an echo-filled beat, or a delay pedal to a synth.

    Improv

    I'm an improviser! I started taking classes in 2023 after my buddy's now-fiance had her graduation show, and I thought "that looks cool, I wanna try!" After a year of classes, I graduated the program. It's really a treat to see other people who've done improv for over a decade innovating in the art-form and making shows really fun to watch. I also have the privilege of occasionally playing with some very talented and fun improvisers. If you're ever in STL, checkout the Improv Shop!
